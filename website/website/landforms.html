GEOGRAPHY


Below are the definitions of several landforms and bodies of water for you to learn about.


Archipelago - A chain of islands in an ocean or sea.

Bay - A body of water that is partly enclosed by land.

Canyon - A deep valley with very steep sides.

Cape - A pointed piece of land that sticks out into a body of water.

Cave - A large hole in the ground or in the side of a hill or mountain.

Channel - A body of water that connects two larger bodies of water.

Cliff - A steep face of rock and soil.

Desert - A very dry area of land.

Estuary - The place when a river meats the sea or ocean.

Fjord - A long narrow sea inlet that is bordered by steep cliffs.

Glacier - A long-lasting, slowly-moving river of ice.

Gulf - Part of an ocean or sea that is partly surrounded by land. A gulf is usually larger than a bay.

Island - A piece of land surrounded by water on all sides.

Isthmus - A narrow strip of land that connects two larger pieces of land. It is surrounded by water on two sides.

Key - A low-lying island, usually formed by a reef.

Lake - A large body of water surrounded by land on all sides.

Mountain - A very tall, natural piece of land.

Ocean - A large body of salt water that borders continents and other pieces of land.

Peninsula - A piece of land surrounded by water on three sides.

Plain - A piece of flat land with small changes in elevation.

Pond - A small body of water surrounded by land on all sides.

River - A large, flowing body of water that usually empties into an ocean or sea.

Sea - A very large lake that is usually connected to an ocean or another body of water that leads out to an ocean. They are also known as very large lakes.

Strait - A narrow body of water that connects two larger bodies of water.

Tundra - A cold, treeless area, located in the far north, south, and at high altitudes.

Valley - A low place between mountains.
